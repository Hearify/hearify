Can you name the capital of Kazakhstan? What about the capital of Myanmar — is it still Rangoon, or did it change? This quiz puts your world map knowledge to the test with 25 questions covering capital cities from every continent, ranging from the well-known to the genuinely surprising.

## Why Capital Cities?

Capital cities are more than administrative centers — they're often the cultural, political, and economic heart of their nations. Knowing world capitals is a foundation for understanding geopolitics, international news, and global culture.

This quiz also exposes some fascinating quirks:
- Countries with two capitals (South Africa has three)
- Capitals that changed recently (Myanmar moved its capital in 2006)
- Tiny capitals of huge countries (Canberra serves all of Australia)
- Capitals that most people confuse with the country's largest city (Wellington, not Auckland, is New Zealand's capital)

## Sample Questions

**Easy:** What is the capital of France?
*(Paris)*

**Medium:** What is the capital of Australia?
*(Canberra — not Sydney, which is the largest city)*

**Hard:** What is the capital of Kazakhstan?
*(Astana — renamed from Nur-Sultan in 2022, and before that Astana, before that Akmola)*

**Very Hard:** What is the capital of Nauru?
*(Yaren — though technically it's a district, not a formal city capital)*

## Did You Know?

- **Brazil moved its capital** from Rio de Janeiro to Brasília in 1960 — a planned city built from scratch
- **Myanmar built a new capital** called Naypyidaw in 2006, replacing Yangon (Rangoon), reportedly because the old capital was seen as strategically vulnerable
- **South Africa has three capitals**: Pretoria (executive), Cape Town (legislative), and Bloemfontein (judicial)
- **The smallest capital in Europe** is Vaduz, Liechtenstein, with a population of around 5,000
